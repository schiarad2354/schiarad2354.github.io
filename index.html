Objectives

Cybersecurity personnel remain overwhelmed with high workloads and burnout, making it difficult for effective cybercrime detection, prevention, and management. This leaves agencies such as the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) requiring adaptable tools for their cybercrime-related operations to better effectuate appropriate responses. That is why, this work studies the geographical significance of various socio-economic and technological determinants that are associated with cybercrime activities across time. In particular, this study analyzes the cybercrime severity of each state and FBI main field office jurisdiction in the contiguous U.S. from 2013 to 2022 by leveraging comprehensive cybercrime and socio-economic data.
